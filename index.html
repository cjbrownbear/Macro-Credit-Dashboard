Macro–Credit Stress Dashboard

Tracks a few stress-sensitive signals that often move early when households get squeezed: card delinquencies, charge-offs, debt-service burden (TDSP), and labor demand (job openings). Tiles show the latest value, a 10-year baseline, and the 1-year change with clear units. Charts below show the full trend with hover-to-inspect values.

Trends (interactive)

Card 30+ Delinquency (All banks)
FRED: DRCCLACBS (quarterly, %)
Share of card balances that are at least 30 days past due — an early consumer stress signal.
Net Charge-off Rate (All banks)
FRED: CORCCACBS (quarterly, %)
Portion of card loans written off as uncollectible — tends to lag delinquencies but confirms credit deterioration.
Debt Service Burden (Households)
FRED: TDSP (quarterly, %)
Household debt payments as a % of disposable income — a “squeeze” measure that can rise even before delinquencies spike.
Job Openings (Total nonfarm)
FRED: JTSJOL (monthly, millions)
Labor demand proxy — weakens as hiring appetite drops (lower is worse for the labor market).
Revolving Consumer Credit
FRED: REVOLSL (monthly, $ billions)
Total revolving consumer credit outstanding — useful context, but not a pure “stress” KPI by itself.
