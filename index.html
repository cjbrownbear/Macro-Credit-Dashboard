Macro–Credit Stress Dashboard

A weekly snapshot of consumer credit stress and labor softening using public macro series (FRED). Tiles show the latest value, a 10-year baseline, and the 1-year change with clear units. Hover charts to inspect point-in-time values. Data loads from data.json and news.json.

Trends (interactive)

Card 30+ Delinquency (All banks)
FRED: DRCCLACBS (quarterly, %)
Share of card balances 30+ days past due — early consumer stress signal.
Net Charge-off Rate (All banks)
FRED: CORCCACBS (quarterly, %)
Portion of card loans written off as uncollectible — confirms deterioration.
Revolving Consumer Credit
FRED: REVOLSL (monthly, $ billions)
Outstanding revolving consumer credit — rising levels can amplify stress if incomes soften.
Debt Service Burden (Households)
FRED: TDSP (quarterly, %)
Household debt payments as % of disposable income — “squeeze” measure.
Job Openings (Total nonfarm)
FRED: JTSJOL (monthly, millions)
Labor demand proxy — a sustained drop usually precedes weaker hiring.
Mortgage 30+ Delinquency (All banks)
FRED: DRSFRMACBS (quarterly, %)
Share of mortgage balances 30+ days past due — housing stress proxy.
Unemployment Rate
FRED: UNRATE (monthly, %)
Broad unemployment measure — tends to lag, but persistent rises worsen credit performance.
Initial Jobless Claims
FRED: ICSA (weekly, thousands)
Faster-turn labor stress signal — spikes can precede unemployment increases.
